Research Projects at SRM-ATMOS

SRM-ATMOS conducts interdisciplinary research on weather and climate extremes using atmospheric observations, satellite data, numerical modelling, climate simulations and artificial intelligence. The projects address scientific challenges associated with high-impact weather events and climate variability over India and surrounding regions.

Ongoing Research Projects

Extreme Rainfall over Southern Peninsular India

Investigation of extreme rainfall events using observations, ERA5 reanalysis and high-resolution WRF simulations.

Physics-Informed AI for Rainfall Forecasting

Development of physics-informed artificial intelligence frameworks for rainfall prediction and weather forecasting.

Lightning Hazard Prediction

Analysis of lightning activity and development of hazard prediction methodologies using observational datasets and modelling approaches.

Atmospheric Electricity Studies

Investigation of electric field variability, aerosol interactions and atmospheric electrification processes.

Climate and Modelling Projects

CMIP6 Climate Projections

Assessment of future climate extremes, monsoon variability and precipitation changes using CMIP6 climate simulations.

WRF Numerical Modelling

High-resolution simulations of weather systems, thunderstorms and rainfall extremes using the WRF model.

Thunderstorm Electrification

Understanding thunderstorm dynamics and lightning generation using modelling and observations.

Satellite Meteorology Applications

Use of INSAT, GPM and MODIS observations for weather and climate monitoring.

Research Methodologies

  • Atmospheric Observations
  • Satellite Remote Sensing
  • ERA5 Reanalysis
  • Numerical Weather Prediction (WRF)
  • Climate Modelling (CMIP6)
  • Lightning Observations (WWLLN)
  • Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
  • Physics-Informed Neural Networks

Expected Outcomes

  • Improved understanding of extreme rainfall mechanisms.
  • Enhanced lightning hazard prediction capability.
  • High-resolution weather and climate datasets.
  • Physics-informed AI frameworks for forecasting.
  • Improved prediction of weather and climate extremes.
  • Scientific publications and trained research personnel.
