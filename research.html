Atmospheric Science Research Laboratory

Weather and Climate Extremes Research Group

Research Overview

SRM-ATMOS conducts interdisciplinary research in atmospheric sciences by integrating atmospheric observations, satellite remote sensing, numerical weather prediction, climate modelling and artificial intelligence. The research group focuses on understanding the physical processes governing weather and climate extremes and developing improved forecasting and prediction systems.

Major Research Themes

Weather and Climate Extremes

Investigation of heavy rainfall, floods, droughts, heat waves and other extreme weather events over India with special emphasis on Southern Peninsular India.

Lightning Science

Studies on lightning climatology, lightning hazard prediction, thunderstorm electrification and atmospheric electricity using ground-based and satellite observations.

Numerical Weather Prediction

High-resolution simulations using the Weather Research and Forecasting (WRF) model to investigate mesoscale weather systems and extreme rainfall events.

Satellite Meteorology

Applications of INSAT, GPM, TRMM, MODIS and other Earth observation systems for atmospheric monitoring and weather analysis.

Climate Modelling

Assessment of climate variability, climate change and future climate projections using CMIP6 and regional climate modelling frameworks.

Artificial Intelligence

Development of machine learning and physics-informed artificial intelligence approaches for weather forecasting and climate applications.

Numerical Modelling Research

WRF Modelling

High-resolution simulations of thunderstorms, heavy rainfall events and severe weather systems using the WRF-ARW modelling framework.

Data Assimilation

Integration of observations and numerical models for improved weather prediction and atmospheric diagnostics.

Climate Simulations

Regional climate assessments and evaluation of climate model projections.

Observational Research

Lightning Observations

WWLLN and other lightning datasets for understanding lightning occurrence and severe weather environments.

Atmospheric Monitoring

Measurements using Aethalometer, Sun Photometer, Automatic Weather Station and LIDAR systems.

Satellite Observations

Use of INSAT, GPM and other remote sensing datasets for atmospheric process studies.
