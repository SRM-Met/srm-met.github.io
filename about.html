Laboratory Overview

SRM-ATMOS (Atmospheric Science Research Laboratory) is a research laboratory in the Department of Physics and Nanotechnology at SRM Institute of Science and Technology, Kattankulathur, India. The laboratory focuses on advancing scientific understanding of atmospheric processes, weather and climate extremes, lightning activity, atmospheric electricity, numerical weather prediction and climate variability.

The laboratory integrates observations, satellite remote sensing, reanalysis datasets, numerical modelling and artificial intelligence to investigate high-impact weather and climate events affecting India and surrounding regions.

Vision

To become a leading research centre for weather and climate sciences through innovative research, advanced numerical modelling, satellite observations and artificial intelligence applications for atmospheric prediction and hazard mitigation.

Mission

  • Advance knowledge of weather and climate extremes.
  • Improve understanding of lightning and atmospheric electricity.
  • Develop numerical weather prediction and climate modelling capabilities.
  • Apply artificial intelligence to atmospheric sciences.
  • Train future researchers in weather and climate sciences.
  • Promote outreach and awareness on environmental sustainability.

Research Focus Areas

Weather and Climate Extremes

Extreme rainfall, floods, droughts and climate variability.

Numerical Weather Prediction

WRF modelling and high-resolution weather simulations.

Lightning Science

Lightning climatology, hazard prediction and thunderstorm electrification.

Atmospheric Electricity

Electric field variability and atmospheric electrical processes.

Satellite Meteorology

INSAT, GPM, MODIS and remote sensing applications.

Climate Modelling

CMIP6 simulations and climate change projections.

Artificial Intelligence

Physics-informed AI and machine learning for weather and climate applications.

Research Impact

The laboratory contributes to scientific research through peer-reviewed publications, numerical modelling studies, climate projections, lightning research and interdisciplinary applications of artificial intelligence. Research outcomes support improved understanding and prediction of high-impact weather and climate hazards.
